- Every sentence describing an action names the action and the system/process context, never a person or team as the causal subject — write "the deploy script skipped the health check" not "the on-call engineer skipped the health check."
- No adjectives or adverbs judging competence or diligence (e.g. "carelessly," "should have known," "failed to properly," "simply forgot") — describe what happened and what the available signal was, not the quality of the decision.
- Every individual is referred to by role or system, not by name or "I/we/they" in a way that assigns blame; direct quotes from individuals are attributed only if they add causal information, never to assign fault.
- The timeline section contains only observable, timestamped events (log lines, alerts, deploys, human actions taken) — no inferred motivations, no "X assumed Y" unless "assumed Y" is evidenced by a ticket, message, or doc citation.
- Root cause is stated as a chain of system/process conditions that made the incident possible, not a single triggering human action — a human action can appear as one link in the chain but never as the terminal "root."
- Every contributing factor is paired with a corresponding action item in the remediation section; a factor listed with no linked follow-up is a defect in the doc, not just the response.
- Action items are owned by a role/team and system, phrased as changes to tooling, process, defaults, or documentation — never phrased as "be more careful," "double-check next time," or other exhortations to individual vigilance.
- Severity and impact figures (duration, customers affected, revenue, SLA breach) are stated with source and measurement method, not estimated adjectives like "significant" or "minor" without a number attached.
- Counterfactuals ("if X had checked Y, this wouldn't have happened") are banned from the analysis section; the doc may state what monitoring/tooling would have caught it, but not what a person should have differently done.
- The doc names what went well (detection, mitigation, communication) with the same specificity as what went wrong — a postmortem with only failure analysis and no working-signal analysis is incomplete.
