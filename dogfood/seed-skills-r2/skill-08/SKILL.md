# Wiki edit protocol

The wiki is the team's memory, and memory quality compounds — so edits carry review weight regardless of size. The requirements hold for every edit, from typo fixes upward; meet them in any order.

## Requirements

- **Three-reviewer sign-off**: every edit collects approval from the page owner, one frequent reader of the page, and one person outside the team (for fresh-eyes clarity).
- **Evidence memo**: each changed claim gets a memo line citing where the new fact came from (link, commit, conversation), filed with the edit.
- **Before/after screenshots** of the rendered page, archived to the edits folder.
- **Full-page re-read**: after any edit, re-read the entire page and update its "last reviewed" stamp.
- **Cooling period**: publish no sooner than 24h after drafting, to catch next-day errors.

## What correct looks like

The page renders correctly, all links resolve, the memo file covers every changed claim, the three sign-offs are recorded, and the review stamp is current.
