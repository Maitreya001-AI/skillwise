# User research data handling

Most of this protocol is ordered, and the order is not ours to relax: it comes from the participant agreement and the privacy regulator, not from workflow taste.

- **Consent precedes collection — always.** Recording may not start before the participant has accepted the consent form for *this* study; a prior study's consent does not carry over. This is a legal requirement, not a courtesy.
- **Anonymization precedes sharing.** Findings leave the research group only after names, voices, employers, and incidental identifiers are stripped; the reviewer who checks anonymization must not be the person who did the stripping.
- **Deletion follows retention expiry.** Raw recordings are deleted at 12 months from collection (the retention period participants agreed to); derived anonymized notes may be kept.

## Facts

- Consent forms live in `research/consent/`, one signed copy per participant per study.
- The approved storage location is the research drive; laptops and chat uploads are not storage locations.

## What correct looks like

Before circulating anything: every quoted participant maps to a signed consent form; no identifier survives in the artifact (search for names, employer strings, and voice files); the retention calendar has an entry for every new recording. If any check fails, the artifact does not circulate — there is no editorial override, because the constraint isn't editorial.
